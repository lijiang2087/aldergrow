Thesis

DigitalOcean is the "simple cloud" for developers and SMBs — Droplets, App Platform, managed databases, managed Kubernetes, and Spaces — serving the long tail of builders who want infrastructure without AWS complexity. The company has carved out a durable niche as the "not hyperscaler" option: predictable pricing, clear docs, and a developer-first UX that wins when startups and indie hackers outgrow hobby platforms.

The catalyst: AI/ML workloads — inference, fine-tuning, and training pipelines are increasingly run on GPU instances. DOCN has added GPU Droplets and App Platform support for AI apps. As the AI build wave moves from early adopters to the broader developer base, DOCN's simplicity and price transparency position it as a natural landing spot for teams that don't want to navigate AWS's 200+ services. At 8.4x P/S vs. Cloudflare at 33x, the market is still pricing DOCN as a slower-growth cloud. If AI adoption accelerates in the SMB segment, DOCN could re-rate.

4P Framework

People
CEO Yancey Spruill joined in 2019 from SendGrid and has led the IPO and pivot to profitability. He's focused on land-and-expand: win developers with simple products, then upsell managed services. CFO Matt Steinfort is disciplined on cost structure. Insider ownership is meaningful; management has skin in the game. No major governance red flags.
Product
Core product: Droplets (VMs), App Platform (PaaS), managed databases (Postgres, Redis, Kafka), Spaces (S3-compatible object storage), managed Kubernetes. The stack is intentionally narrow — no 200 services, just what developers need. GPU Droplets for AI/ML workloads are a recent addition. Pricing is transparent and predictable; no surprise bills. NPS is high among developers.
Potential
Revenue growth has been in the mid-teens. The AI/ML segment is early but growing — DOCN is well-positioned for inference workloads that don't need hyperscale. International expansion (India, Singapore) adds TAM. SMB cloud spend is growing faster than enterprise in some segments. If DOCN captures even a small share of the AI builder wave, growth could accelerate. Analysts see ~20% revenue growth in 2026.
Predictability
Mixed. Developer-focused customers are sticky but can be price-sensitive. Churn is manageable; net revenue retention is solid. The shift to managed services (higher-margin) improves unit economics over time. Competition from AWS Lightsail, GCP, and smaller players (Linode, Vultr) is real but DOCN has differentiated on simplicity. Margins are improving; path to sustained profitability is visible.

Key Catalyst

AI Build Wave — GPU & App Platform

DigitalOcean has added GPU Droplets and App Platform support for AI apps. As the AI build wave moves from early adopters (who use hyperscalers) to the broader developer base, DOCN's simplicity becomes a selling point. Teams building AI apps want predictable pricing, clear docs, and a path from prototype to production. DOCN's developer-first positioning and transparent pricing could capture share as AI workloads proliferate.

Valuation Context

Cloudflare (NET) trades at 33x P/S with negative GAAP earnings. Akamai (AKAM) at 3.8x as a legacy CDN being repriced. DOCN at 8.4x after a strong YTD run — the multiple reflects the "simple cloud" narrative but not yet AI upside. If AI/ML becomes a meaningful revenue driver, DOCN could justify 10–12x P/S. If growth stays mid-teens, the current multiple is fair. The key is whether AI adoption accelerates in the SMB segment.
