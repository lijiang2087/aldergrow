# AI Investing Guide: A Framework for Evaluating AI Companies

---

The AI sector moves fast. Public markets and private rounds both reward narrative as often as fundamentals. This framework is meant to help you think clearly before you allocate capital — whether you’re buying a single stock or building a thematic sleeve.

## 1. Clarify what you’re actually buying

**Infrastructure vs. application vs. picks-and-shovels.** Is the company selling compute, models, data, or software that sits on top? Margins and defensibility look very different across these layers.

**Revenue quality.** Is growth recurring (subscriptions, enterprise contracts) or one-off (implementation, consulting)? AI hype has inflated many “AI revenue” lines that are mostly services.

**Customer concentration.** A few big logos can look impressive but concentration risk is real. Ask: what happens if the top two customers churn or renegotiate?

## 2. Due diligence checklist

- **Unit economics:** Gross margin trend, not just headline revenue growth.
- **Moat:** Proprietary data, distribution, switching costs, or brand — not just “we use GPT.”
- **R&D vs. marketing:** Heavy spend on ads with thin product differentiation is a yellow flag.
- **Regulatory and IP:** Training data, copyright, and export controls can flip a thesis quickly.
- **Management:** Prior exits and capital allocation matter more in a hype cycle than in mature sectors.

## 3. Red flags

- **Vague “AI” labeling** with no clear product or measurable outcome for customers.
- **Guidance that only goes up** with no discussion of competition or margin pressure.
- **Insider selling** clustered around promotional news cycles (context matters, but worth watching).
- **Dependence on a single model provider** with no alternative or negotiation leverage.

## 4. Portfolio construction

- **Size positions** so a single thesis failure doesn’t derail your plan.
- **Mix layers:** don’t put everything in one sub-theme (e.g. only chip names or only chatbots).
- **Rebalance** when narrative and price diverge — AI names can mean-revert violently after runs.

## 5. What to do next

Use this as a scratchpad, not a scorecard. No framework catches every winner or every fraud. Pair it with your own research, position sizing, and time horizon.
